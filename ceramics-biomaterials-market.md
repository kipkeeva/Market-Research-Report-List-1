<p><strong>The Ceramics Biomaterials Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Bio-Inert Ceramics</li><li>Bioactive Ceramics</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Ceramics biomaterials are divided into two main types: bio-inert ceramics and bioactive ceramics. Bio-inert ceramics have excellent mechanical properties and are chemically stable in the body, making them ideal for applications that require high strength and low reactivity, such as orthopedic implants. On the other hand, bioactive ceramics can bond directly with living tissues and promote bone growth, making them suitable for applications like bone grafts and dental implants. These ceramics interact with the body's natural processes, enhancing tissue regeneration and reducing the risk of rejection or complications.</p></p>
