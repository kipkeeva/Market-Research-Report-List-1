<p><p>The E Glass Fiber Yarn market consists of two main types: Fiber Yarn and Fiber Roving. Fiber Yarn refers to continuous strands of E Glass Fiber that are twisted together to form a yarn-like structure. This type of yarn is commonly used in the manufacturing of textiles, fabrics, and reinforcements for composites. On the other hand, Fiber Roving involves multiple strands of E Glass Fiber that are loosely assembled together. This type of roving is primarily used in applications where bulk and thickness are desired, such as for insulation materials or molding processes.</p></p>
