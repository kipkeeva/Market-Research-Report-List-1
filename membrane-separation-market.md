<p><p>Membrane separation is a technique used to separate different components in a mixture by utilizing a semi-permeable membrane. There are various types of membrane separation processes including Reverse Osmosis (RO), Ultrafiltration (UF), Microfiltration (MF), Nanofiltration (NF), and others. RO is primarily used for desalination and purification of water, while UF is effective in removing large particles and macromolecules. MF is suitable for separating smaller particles, while NF is commonly used for removing organic and inorganic compounds. Other types of membrane separations include electrodialysis and pervaporation, used for specific applications like desalting and volatile-component removal.</p></p>
