<p><ul><li>Long (bast) Fibers</li><li>Short (core) Fibers</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Flax fiber market types encompass long (bast) fibers, short (core) fibers, and other variations. Long fibers are derived from the phloem or bark of the flax plant, known for their strength and flexibility, and are mainly used for producing linen textiles. Short fibers come from the central woody core of the flax stem, possessing less strength and mainly used for non-woven products such as insulation or composites. Other market variations may include processed flax fibers or specialty products to cater to specific applications, enhancing the versatility of flax in various industries.</p></p>
