<p><p>Alnico magnets are classified into three main types based on their manufacturing processes: cast alnico, sintered alnico, and bar alnico. Cast alnico magnets are made by pouring molten alloy into a mold, allowing for complex shapes and sizes. Sintered alnico magnets involve compacting powdered alloy with a pressing tool followed by high-temperature sintering. They exhibit better mechanical properties compared to cast alnico magnets. Lastly, bar alnico magnets are made by either casting or sintering methods and are formed into a bar shape, commonly used for educational or industrial purposes.</p></p>
