<p><p>Hydroponic growth medium refers to the substrate or material used to support the roots of plants in hydroponic systems. Unlike traditional soil-based agriculture, hydroponics allows plants to grow in nutrient-rich water solutions, eliminating the need for soil altogether. Common mediums used in hydroponics include perlite, coconut coir, vermiculite, and rockwool.</p><p>Hydroponic nutrient solutions, on the other hand, consist of a well-balanced mixture of essential minerals and elements needed for plant growth. These nutrients are dissolved in water and directly provided to the plant roots. The composition of hydroponic nutrients can be tailored to specific crop requirements, resulting in faster growth, higher yields, and better plant nutrition.</p><p>The hydroponic growth medium and nutrient market is expected to experience significant growth in the coming years.
