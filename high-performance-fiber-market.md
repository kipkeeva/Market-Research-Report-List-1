<p><p>The high-performance fiber market is divided into various types such as carbon fiber, aramid, PBI, PPS, glass fiber, high-strength polyethylene, and others. Carbon fiber exhibits exceptional strength and stiffness, making it highly suitable for aerospace and automotive applications. Aramid fibers possess high tensile strength and heat resistance, commonly used in ballistic protection and tire reinforcement. PBI fibers offer excellent flame resistance and thermal stability, prevalent in firefighters' gear and aircraft interiors. PPS fibers are known for their chemical resistance and electrical properties, often found in electrical insulation and filter media. Glass fibers offer low cost and versatile applications in construction and automotive sectors. High-strength polyethylene fibers possess excellent impact resistance and are utilized in body armor and ropes. Lastly, "others" encompass fibers such as nylon, polyester, and basalt used in various industries.</p></p>
