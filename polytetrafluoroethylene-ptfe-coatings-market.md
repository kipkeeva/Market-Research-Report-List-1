<p><p>Polytetrafluoroethylene (PTFE) coatings are widely used in various industries due to their exceptional nonstick properties and high resistance to heat, chemicals, and weathering. The market for PTFE coatings is categorized into two types: powder coating and liquid coating. Powder coating involves electrostatically applying a dry powder onto the substrate and then curing it in an oven, resulting in a smooth, durable, and uniform coating. On the other hand, liquid coating involves applying a liquid PTFE coating directly onto the substrate and allowing it to dry or cure, providing a thin yet protective layer. Both powder and liquid coatings offer excellent adhesion, uniformity, and corrosion resistance.</p></p>
