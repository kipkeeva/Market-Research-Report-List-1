<p><strong>The Low Calorie Foods Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Sugar Substitutes</li><li>Sugar Alcohol Substitutes</li><li>Nutrient Based Substitutes</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The low calorie foods market comprises three major types: sugar substitutes, sugar alcohol substitutes, and nutrient-based substitutes. Sugar substitutes are artificial sweeteners that provide sweetness without adding calories to food and beverages. Sugar alcohol substitutes, such as erythritol or xylitol, are sweeteners derived from fruits or plants that contain fewer calories and have a minimal impact on blood sugar levels. Nutrient-based substitutes, like stevia, are natural sweeteners extracted from plants that offer little to no calories while adding flavor to food and drinks.</p></p>
