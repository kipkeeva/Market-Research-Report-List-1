<p><p>The radiation shielding lead glass market offers various types of lead glass with different lead equivalence measurements. Lead equivalence is a measure of how well the glass can absorb and block radiation. For example, glass with a lead equivalence of 1.8-2.3/7mm~8mm means that it can effectively shield against radiation with the same level of protection as 1.8 to 2.3mm of pure lead, and the glass thickness ranges from 7mm to 8mm. Similarly, other lead equivalence measurements and corresponding glass thicknesses are available, such as 2.2-3.3/10mm~12mm, 3.6-4.8/15mm~18mm, and 4.4-5.4/20mm. These lead glasses are used in various industries that require radiation shielding, such as medical facilities, nuclear power plants, and research laboratories.</p></p>
