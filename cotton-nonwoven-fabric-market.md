<p><ul><li>Cross Layer Type</li><li>Parallel Type</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The cotton nonwoven fabric market includes two types: Cross Layer Type and Parallel Type. Cross Layer Type refers to fabrics that are made by overlapping or layering cotton fibers in different directions, creating a cross pattern. This type of fabric offers high strength and stability. On the other hand, Parallel Type fabrics are made by aligning cotton fibers in the same direction. They provide good filtration and absorption properties. Both types of cotton nonwoven fabrics have various applications in industries such as healthcare, automotive, and construction.</p></p>
