<p><p>Magnesium Hydroxide is produced through two primary methods: chemical synthesis and physical method. In the chemical synthesis method, magnesium oxide reacts with water or a magnesium salt to form magnesium hydroxide. This process usually involves the utilization of synthetic chemicals and is commonly used in industrial applications. On the other hand, the physical method entails the precipitation of magnesium hydroxide from a magnesium salt solution by adding a strong base or an alkali. This method is often employed in the production of pharmaceutical grade magnesium hydroxide.</p></p>
