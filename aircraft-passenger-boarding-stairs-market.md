<p><p>In the aircraft passenger boarding stairs market, there are two main types: towered and self-propelled stairs. Towered stairs are typically operated by a ground crew or ground support equipment. They are connected to a stationary tower structure to provide access to the aircraft. On the other hand, self-propelled stairs are equipped with their own power source, allowing them to operate independently. They can be moved and positioned without the need for a fixed tower structure, providing more flexibility in boarding and disembarking passengers.</p></p>
