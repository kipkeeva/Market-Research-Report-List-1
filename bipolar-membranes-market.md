Homogeneous bipolar membranes are made of a single material and provide uniform ion transport, while heterogeneous bipolar membranes consist of different materials arranged in layers, enabling selective ion transport. The choice between these types depends on specific application requirements, such as desired ion separation and efficiency. Both types play a crucial role in facilitating important electrochemical processes in industries like energy, water treatment, and chemical production.</p></p>
