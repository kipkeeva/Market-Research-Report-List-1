<p><p>Polypropylene, a thermoplastic polymer, has gained significant popularity in the 3D printing market due to its versatility and cost-effectiveness. There are three main types of polypropylene used in this industry: Isotactic, Atactic, and Syndiotactic. Isotactic PP is the most common, known for its crystalline structure, high stiffness, and strength. Atactic PP is amorphous, offering better flexibility and impact resistance. Syndiotactic PP, with its crystalline structure, falls between the isotactic and atactic types, providing a balance of strength and flexibility. Each type caters to different 3D printing requirements, expanding the potential applications in various industries.</p></p>
