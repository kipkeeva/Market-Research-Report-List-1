<p><p>Emulsified asphalt is a type of asphalt that is mixed with water and an emulsifying agent to form a stable suspension. Anionic emulsified asphalt is negatively charged and is commonly used in cold mix applications. Cationic emulsified asphalt is positively charged and is widely used in hot mix applications. Other markets for emulsified asphalt include applications in surface treatments, crack sealing, and chip sealing. These different types cater to various construction needs and offer different performance characteristics, ensuring versatility and flexibility in road construction and maintenance projects.</p></p>
