<p><p>Fiberglass roving is a widely used material in various industries. Single-end roving refers to a continuous strand of fiberglass yarn wound on a bobbin, primarily used for filament winding and pultrusion processes. Multi-end roving consists of several strands wound together, provided in different tex sizes, and used for applications like sheet molding compounds and compression molding. Chopped roving comprises small lengths of fiberglass strands cut into specific sizes, used in applications like injection molding and thermoplastics. These different market types cater to distinct manufacturing processes and end-use requirements within the fiberglass industry.</p></p>
