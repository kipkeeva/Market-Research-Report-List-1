<p><p>Primary antioxidants are substances that help in preventing the oxidation process in various materials, such as polymers and fuels, by inhibiting the formation of free radicals. Amines are a type of primary antioxidant that function by donating hydrogen atoms to neutralize free radicals. Hindered phenols, another type of primary antioxidant, work by trapping free radicals and breaking the oxidation chain reaction. Both amines and hindered phenols are widely used across industries like plastics, rubber, and automotive to enhance product durability and stability by protecting against oxidation.</p></p>
