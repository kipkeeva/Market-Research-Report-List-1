<p><p>Architectural toughened glass, also known as tempered glass, is a type of safety glass that is processed by controlled thermal or chemical treatments to increase its strength compared to normal glass. Toughened glass is manufactured by heating the glass to around 620 degrees Celsius and then rapidly cooling it with compressed air. This process creates tension within the glass, resulting in increased strength and ability to withstand high levels of mechanical and thermal stress.</p><p>The architectural toughened glass market is driven by the increasing demand for safety and security features in buildings, as well as the growing construction industry worldwide. Toughened glass offers various advantages, such as resistance to breakage, high durability, and increased thermal stability, making it a preferred choice for architectural applications.</p><p>In terms of market analysis, the architectural toughened glass market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 4.6% during the forecast period.
