<p><p>EMI materials refer to substances used to shield electronic devices from electromagnetic interference. Polymer EMI materials are made from conductive polymers and are known for their flexibility, thermal stability, and lightweight properties. They are used in various applications, including consumer electronics and automotive sectors. On the other hand, metal EMI materials are composed of metallic elements such as iron, nickel, or copper. They offer superior shielding effectiveness but are heavier and less flexible than polymer materials. Metal EMI materials are widely used in industries that require robust electromagnetic interference protection, such as aerospace and military applications.</p></p>
