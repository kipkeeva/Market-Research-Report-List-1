<p><p>Electronic special gases for etching are essential in the semiconductor industry for various etching processes. Fluorinated carbon gases, such as CF4 and C4F8, are widely used for dry etching due to their high etch rates and selectivity. Chlorine-based gases, such as Cl2 and HCl, are known for their high reactivity and are utilized for isotropic etching. Oxygen gases, like O2 and CO2, are often employed for oxide etching. Other gases, such as SF6 and N2, are used for specific applications like silicon etching or plasma cleaning. These gases play a crucial role in achieving precise and controlled etching in semiconductor fabrication.</p></p>
