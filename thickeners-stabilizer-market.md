<p><p>Thickeners stabilizers are substances used to enhance the texture, consistency, and stability of various food and industrial products. There are different types of thickeners stabilizers in the market, including gelatin, guar gum, agar, synthetic cellulose, and others. Gelatin is derived from animal bones and provides a smooth and elastic texture. Guar gum, extracted from the guar bean, is known for its excellent water-binding properties. Agar is a seaweed extract that forms a gel-like substance and helps in stabilizing emulsions. Synthetic cellulose, derived from wood pulp, is commonly used in processed foods. Other types of thickeners stabilizers include starches, pectin, and carrageenan, each offering unique properties to enhance product quality and stability.</p></p>
