<p><p>Fused zirconia, also known as fused zirconium dioxide, is a high-performance ceramic material that is derived from zircon sand. It is produced by fusing zirconium sand with an amorphous silica source at very high temperatures. Fused zirconia exhibits excellent physical and chemical properties, including high thermal shock resistance, low thermal conductivity, high mechanical strength, and resistance to corrosion and wear. These properties make fused zirconia ideal for use in a wide range of applications such as refractories, abrasives, ceramics, and electronics.</p><p>The global fused zirconia market is expected to experience significant growth in the coming years. <p><p>The Fused Zirconia market consists of two types: Electrically Fused Zirconia and Chemical Synthesis of Zirconia. Electrically Fused Zirconia is created by subjecting zirconium oxide to high temperatures using an electric arc furnace. This process helps achieve high purity and fine grain size zirconia, making it suitable for various applications in industries like aerospace and electronics. On the other hand, Chemical Synthesis of Zirconia involves the reaction of zirconium compounds with other chemicals to produce zirconia particles. This method allows for precise control over particle size and morphology, offering customization options for specific purposes.</p></p>
