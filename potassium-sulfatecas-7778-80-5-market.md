The market for potassium sulfate can be categorized into three main types: the Mannheim process, MOP (Muriate of Potash) and Kieserite process, and brines (salt lakes) processing. The Mannheim process involves the reaction between potassium chloride and sulfuric acid. MOP and Kieserite process refer to the extraction of potassium and sulfur from natural minerals. Lastly, brines processing involves the extraction of potassium sulfate from salt lakes through evaporation.</p></p>
