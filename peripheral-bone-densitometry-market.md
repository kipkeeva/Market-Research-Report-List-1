<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>Peripheral bone densitometry market includes various types of technologies used for evaluating bone density and diagnosing osteoporosis. Single Energy X-Ray Absorptiometry (SEXA) measures bone mineral density using low-dose X-ray beams. Dual Energy X-Ray Absorptiometry (pDEXA) provides better accuracy by differentiating between soft tissue and bone density. Radiographic Absorptiometry (RA) measures bone density using X-rays. Quantitative Ultrasound (QUS) uses sound waves to measure bone density. Peripheral Quantitative Computed Tomography (pQCT) utilizes X-rays to create cross-sectional images of bone. Each technology offers different advantages and is suitable for specific patient populations.</p></p>
