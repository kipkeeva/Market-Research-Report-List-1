<p><p>Phenolic adhesives are classified into two main types: granular resin and flaky resin. Granular resin refers to phenolic adhesives that are in the form of small granules that can be easily melted and applied to various materials. Flaky resin, on the other hand, is composed of thin flakes that can be easily mixed with solvents to create adhesives of desired thickness and consistency. Both types of phenolic adhesives offer excellent bonding properties and are widely used in various industrial applications.</p></p>
