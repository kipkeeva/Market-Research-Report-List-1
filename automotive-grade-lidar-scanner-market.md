<p><p>The Automotive Grade LiDAR Scanner market includes two main types: Mechanical LiDAR and Solid State LiDAR. Mechanical LiDAR scanners rely on moving parts, such as rotating mirrors, to emit and detect laser beams, providing accurate distance measurements. On the other hand, Solid State LiDAR scanners are built with no moving parts, utilizing advanced photonics technologies like MEMS mirrors. They offer compactness, improved reliability, and durability, making them ideal for automotive applications. Both types play a vital role in enabling autonomous driving by providing essential sensor data.</p></p>
