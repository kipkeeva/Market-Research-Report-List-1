Halogenated flame retardants have been found to persist in the environment, pose potential health hazards, and contribute to the formation of dioxins and other toxic byproducts when burned.</p><p>The current outlook for the halogen free flame retardant market is highly positive. The increasing regulatory restrictions on halogenated flame retardants, coupled with the rising awareness about the environmental and health risks associated with them, are driving the demand for halogen free alternatives. Aluminum Hydroxide is a commonly used flame retardant that releases water vapor to dilute gases during combustion. Magnesium Hydroxide functions by releasing water vapor and decomposing into magnesium oxide, creating a protective layer. Nitrogen compounds prevent the combustion by lowering the oxygen concentration in the surrounding area. Zinc compounds work by releasing zinc oxide, which absorbs heat and hinders combustion. Other types of flame retardants may incorporate phosphorus, boron, or silicon compounds to inhibit fire.</p></p>
