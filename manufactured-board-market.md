<p><p>Manufactured boards, such as plywood and oriented strand board (OSB), are widely used in the construction and furniture industries. Plywood is made by layering thin sheets of wood veneer and bonding them together, resulting in improved strength and stability. OSB, on the other hand, is produced by compressing wood strands with adhesive resin. While both materials offer cost-effectiveness and versatility, plywood is often preferred for its superior quality and aesthetic appeal, whereas OSB is valued for its strength and suitability for structural applications.</p></p>
